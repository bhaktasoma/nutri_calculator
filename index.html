How it works

This calculator estimates your daily energy needs and macro targets using:

  • BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): energy required at rest
  • Activity factor: adjusts BMR for your daily exercise and movement
  • Macro percentages: a balanced daily breakdown for protein, fat, and carbs

Formula details

Step 1: Convert units

  • Weight in kg = weight (lbs) × 0.453592
  • Height in cm = feet × 30.48 + inches × 2.54

Step 2: Calculate BMR

Mifflin-St Jeor (used by default):
BMR = 10 × weight_kg + 6.25 × height_cm - 5 × age - 161 (female)
BMR = 10 × weight_kg + 6.25 × height_cm - 5 × age + 5 (male)

Katch-McArdle (used when body fat % is provided):
BMR = 370 + 21.6 × lean_mass_kg
lean_mass_kg = weight_kg × (1 - body_fat_pct / 100)

Step 3: Adjust for activity

TDEE = BMR × activity factor

Common factors:

  • 1.2 sedentary
  • 1.375 lightly active
  • 1.55 moderately active
  • 1.725 very active
  • 1.9 extremely active

Step 4: Build macros

Protein = 25% of calories ÷ 4 kcal/g
Fat = 30% of calories ÷ 9 kcal/g
Carbs = 45% of calories ÷ 4 kcal/g

This is a guideline for daily maintenance. Adjust calories and macros based on your goal:

  • Maintenance: use TDEE as-is with a balanced macro split.
  • Fat loss / Shredding: reduce about 10–15% from TDEE and raise protein slightly.
  • Lean muscle build: increase about 5–10% from TDEE and keep protein high with moderate carbs.
  • Bulking: increase about 10–15% from TDEE and support growth with slightly higher carbs.

Always treat this as an estimate and check with a qualified dietitian for personalized advice.
